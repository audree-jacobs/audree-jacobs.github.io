Design Principles Document

Audrey Jacobs

Visual Hierarchy

Website: The Metropolitan Museum of Art

https://www.metmuseum.org/

This website is a great example of textual visual hierarchy. The first thing you look at on the page is the main title which says "Membership Starts Here". Fro there you're led to the paragraph, then the image, then back up to the bright red menu. The size of the first heading draws you to it, and then proximity leads you to the paragraph and the image, and then color contrast brings attention to the menu. This is how the hierarchy works and moves your eye around the page, starting with the most important heading.

White Space and Clean Design

Website: Prada

https://www.prada.com/us/en.html

The bottom section of this website I thought was a good example of clean design and using white space. The different sections at the bottom are all formatted in the same, simple, black and white format, and the logos for the social media are also all black and white. This simplifies the content which is actually a lot going on but the clean way it's formatted makes it simple and almost boring to look at, so it doesn't look to complicated. There's also a generous amount of space from each line to the + symbol and inbetween lines and logos, so nothing feels crowded.

Repitition

Website: Depop

https://www.depop.com/

This is my favorite example because I just think they did a realy great job and it looks really pretty. The repitition of the color purple from the image to the text box underneath it gives it a cohesive look. The main heading font is the same font as the logo at the top of the page which adds another layer of repitition. All together the design looks very unified and purposeful, and the colors espcially give it a really cool look.
